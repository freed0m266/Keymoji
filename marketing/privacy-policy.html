Keybo — Privacy Policy

Last updated: 24 May 2026

1. What we collect

Nothing. Keybo is a custom iOS keyboard that runs entirely on your device. We do not collect, transmit, store, or share any data — including:

  • Text you type or have typed in any app
  • Words, phrases, or patterns from your input
  • Personal information, device identifiers, or usage analytics
  • Network requests of any kind — Keybo has no internet access by design

2. Why does Keybo ask for "Allow Full Access"?

iOS requires custom keyboards to enable "Allow Full Access" in order to use the haptic feedback API. This is an iOS sandbox restriction, not a data-collection mechanism. Keybo uses Full Access exclusively for:

  • Haptic feedback on key taps
  • Reading/writing keyboard preferences (e.g. "Always show number row") in a shared container between the host app and the keyboard extension

Keybo does not use Full Access for network access, keychain access, or any other purpose.

3. Third-party services

Keybo uses no third-party services, SDKs, or analytics frameworks.

4. Data sharing

Keybo does not share any data with any party because there is no data to share.

5. Children's privacy

Keybo does not collect data from any user, including children under 13.

6. Changes to this policy

If Keybo's behavior ever changes in a way that affects privacy (e.g. adding an optional cloud sync feature), this policy will be updated and the change will be highlighted in the app's release notes.
